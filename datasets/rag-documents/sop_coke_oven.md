# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): Coke Oven Operations & Gas Extraction
Document ID: SOP-COB-01
Version: 4.2
Last Revised: January 15, 2026

## 1. Overview
The Coke Oven Battery (COB) produces high-temperature fuel coke by heating coal in the absence of oxygen. This process releases highly volatile and combustible coke oven gas. Safe extraction of this gas requires continuous mechanical ventilation and atmospheric monitoring.

## 2. Threshold Safety Controls
1. **Combustible Gas Levels (LEL)**:
   - Warning alert at 20% LEL.
   - Critical evacuation alert at 40% LEL.
2. **Ventilation Performance**:
   - Extraction fans must operate above 75% flow efficiency at all times.
   - Efficiency below 70% requires immediate operator dispatch.
   - Efficiency below 50% requires suspension of all active Hot Work permits in the zone.

## 3. Simultaneous Operations (SIMOPS)
- Hot Work (welding, cutting, grinding) is strictly prohibited in the Coke Oven Battery if ventilation efficiency drops below 70% OR combustible gas is detected above 15% LEL.
- If ventilation efficiency drops below 50%, the control room operator must initiate an emergency permit suspension and safety hold on all active operations within the zone.
